A computer in its simplest form is a calculator, and can do everything a basic calculator can(+, -, ×, ÷). The only difference between your calculator and Python is that we use * for multiplication (×) and / for division (÷).

Just like humans, computers follow BODMAS. Bracket, Of, Division, Multiplication, Addition, and Subtraction

Let’s look at a few examples:

>>> (10+10) / 2                    #Output:10.0
10.0
>>> (10+10)*2                      #Output:40
40
>>> 10 + 10 - 5                    #Output:15
15

You must be wondering what’s after # and why it’s used. That’s known as a comment, it is used to explain whatever is written in the program by the programmer, the computer ignores everything after # in that line of the program.

We have two more functions in programming:

  1. Modulus: it is represented with %. It gives the remainder when you are dividing two numbers. Example: 5%2 = 1 because when 5 is divided by 2, 1 is the remainder.
  2. Power: it is represented with ** which puts an exponent on the base. Example: 52 will be written as 5**2 which will give the value of 25.

Example:

>>> 5%2       #Output: 1; (5÷2 = 2 remainder 1!)
1
>>> 5**2      #Output: 25; (52 = 25) 
25
